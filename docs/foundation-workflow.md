# Foundation Workflow

This is the practical project path for turning a BOXABL concept into a buildable foundation package.

## 1. Confirm the Manufacturer Inputs

Lock down the exact:

- model
- revision
- official manual
- current specs
- approved connection details

Do not start with assumptions from a screenshot, reseller post, or outdated PDF.

## 2. Screen the Site and the Jurisdiction

Confirm:

- project address or parcel
- local AHJ
- code cycle in force
- zoning or placement constraints
- flood, drainage, and access issues
- whether the delivery path is physically possible

This is where many "simple prefab" projects become real site projects.

## 3. Collect Soils and Environmental Inputs

At minimum, identify:

- bearing assumptions
- fill conditions
- frost depth
- slope
- groundwater or dewatering risk
- wind exposure
- seismic considerations

If the AHJ or site conditions require it, get the geotechnical report before finalizing the foundation family.

## 4. Choose the Foundation Family

Public examples described in this repo:

- stem wall
- concrete slab
- concrete pier and wood beam
- grade beam

Selection depends on more than cost. It depends on site grading, elevation control, utilities, soil behavior, and how the BOXABL unit needs to bear and connect.

## 5. Coordinate Utilities, Elevations, and Interface Details

Before permit, you need alignment on:

- finished floor elevation
- utility entry points
- plumbing routing
- electrical service approach
- HVAC equipment location
- drainage and water management
- how the unit interfaces with the support condition

This is where field mistakes become expensive.

## 6. Prepare the Site-Specific Engineering Package

A serious package usually needs:

- foundation plan
- sections and details
- bearing assumptions or geotech basis
- anchorage and connection logic
- utility coordination notes
- construction notes
- any required PE seal

The public repo stops before the permit-ready sheet set. That is the paid or controlled layer.

## 7. Permit and Review

The owner, builder, and engineer should confirm:

- submittal format
- wet seal expectations
- delegated engineering expectations
- special inspection requirements
- BOXABL document references required by the AHJ

## 8. Build, Inspect, and Certify

Construction should track the approved package, not the public summary.

Inspection and certification normally include:

- excavation and bearing verification
- reinforcement and embedment review
- utility coordination checks
- finished elevation checks
- final support/interface verification

## The Oasis Positioning Layer

Oasis Engineering fits between "I found a BOXABL online" and "I have an approved, buildable foundation path."

That is the value this workflow is designed to make obvious.
