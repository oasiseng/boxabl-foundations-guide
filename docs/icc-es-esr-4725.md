# ICC-ES ESR-4725 Structural Insulated Panel Certification

This page explains the **ICC-ES ESR-4725 certification**, which validates BOXABL's SIP panel structural, thermal, and code compliance.

It exists because this certification is fundamental to why BOXABL units can be code-compliant in jurisdictions adopting the 2021/2018 IBC or IRC.

> **Scope note — read this first.** ESR-4725 certifies the **panels**: their material properties, allowable pressures, axial and shear capacities, and fire/thermal performance. It does **not** assign a miles-per-hour wind rating to a complete BOXABL unit. Unit-level wind speed ratings (the "140 mph Exposure B / 120 mph Exposure C" figures that circulate online) come from BOXABL's **structural plans for a specific unit and roof type**, not from the ESR. The BXB-000009 manual explicitly defers snow and wind loading to the structural plans. Keep the two layers separate when talking to an AHJ.

## What ESR-4725 Covers

The evaluation report assessed and approved:

### Material Composition & Properties

**Panel Structure:**
- factory-laminated sandwich panels
- facing materials: one steel facing + one MgO (magnesium oxide) panel facing
- core material: EPS (expanded polystyrene) foam per ASTM C578 Type I

**Thicknesses Approved:**
- wall panels: 6" (152.4 mm)
- roof panels: 7.5" (190.5 mm)
- floor panels: 7.5" (190.5 mm)

**Steel Facing:**
- yield strength: 50 ksi
- thickness: 24 gauge (0.73 mm)
- finish: G60 galvanization with factory-applied primer
- width: 57"

**MgO Facing:**
- thickness: 0.236" (6 mm)
- compliance: ICC-ES AC386 certified
- width: 48"

**EPS Core:**
- meets ASTM C578 Type I
- flame spread rating: ≤25 (ASTM E84)
- smoke development: ≤450 (ASTM E84)

**Adhesive:**
- Type II Class 2 per ASTM D3498
- ICC-ES AC05 compliant

- overstrength Ω₀: 2.5

- maximum aspect ratio: 1:1

- shear capacity: 127 plf at 1:1 aspect ratio

- overstrength Ω₀: 3.0
- deflection Cd: 4.0

## Why This Certification Matters for Foundation Design

### For Code Compliance

1. **Proven Structural Equivalency:** ESR-4725 demonstrates that BOXABL's SIP panels meet code-required structural performance for floor and roof bending, axial compression, seismic shear walls, and wind pressures at the panel level.

2. **Thermal Code Compliance:** The R-values documented in ESR-4725 support thermal code compliance in many climate zones under the 2021/2018 IBC/IRC. Confirm against the local energy code.

3. **Fire Safety:** The interior finish and thermal barrier compliance means the panels are recognized as code-compliant for fire safety without additional interior treatments.

4. **Permits and AHJ Acceptance:** Building officials can cite ESR-4725 directly when reviewing the panel system. It removes much of the "is this factory-built product acceptable?" discussion — for the panels. Unit-level and site-level questions remain.

### For Foundation Design

1. **Load Transfer Points:** The panel capacity data tells the foundation engineer what the panels can carry and transmit to the foundation, which informs bearing requirements at panel-to-foundation connections, anchorage sizing, and foundation design pressures.

2. **Seismic Connection:** For sites assigned to SDC D–F, the ESR-4725 shearwall parameters (127 plf, R 6.5) drive shear wall detailing at panel edges, rim load requirements, and foundation anchorage spacing and strength.

3. **Wind Load Path:** Wind loads determined for the site (per ASCE 7 and the unit's structural plans) must be carried from the roof through the walls to the foundation. ESR-4725 confirms the panels' allowable pressures; the unit's structural plans confirm the unit-level rating; the foundation design must complete the load path.

4. **Settlement & Differential Movement:** Because the panels are factory-laminated and uniform, the bearing surface must also be as uniform as possible. Non-uniform settlement can crack panels or separate connections — doubly important for dual-module units.

## Limitations & Clarifications

**ESR-4725 covers:**
- the SIP panel material and properties
- panel structural capacities and code compliance
- thermal and fire performance

**ESR-4725 does NOT cover:**
- unit-level wind speed ratings (those come from the structural plans)
- foundation design (site-specific)
- utility penetrations (site-specific)
- connections to foundations or other structures (site-specific)
- local amendments or modifications to the panels
- how the panels are assembled on-site into a complete unit

Those elements are the responsibility of the site-specific engineer, BOXABL, or the installation contractor.
