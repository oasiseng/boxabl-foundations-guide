# ICC-ES ESR-4725 Structural Insulated Panel Certification

This page explains the **ICC-ES ESR-4725 certification**, which validates BOXABL's SIP panel structural, thermal, and code compliance.

It exists because this certification is fundamental to why BOXABL units can be code-compliant in any jurisdiction adopting the 2021/2018 IBC or IRC.

## Official Certification Reference

**ICC-ES ESR-4725 Evaluation Report**

- **Report Number:** ESR-4725
- **Issue Date:** Reissued May 2024, Renewal Cycle: May 2025
- **Holder:** BOXABL
- **Subject:** Boxabl Structural Insulated Panels (SIPs)
- **Jurisdiction:** 2021 IBC and 2021 IRC (also recognized under 2018 versions)
- **Status:** Active and current

## What ESR-4725 Covers

The evaluation report assessed and approved:

### Material Composition & Properties

**Panel Structure:**
- factory-laminated sandwich panels
- facing materials: one steel facing + one MgO (magnesium oxide) panel facing
- core material: EPS (expanded polystyrene) foam per ASTM C578 Type I

**Thicknesses Approved:**
- wall panels: 6" (152.4 mm)
- roof panels: 7.5" (190.5 mm)
- floor panels: 7.5" (190.5 mm)

**Steel Facing:**
- yield strength: 50 ksi
- thickness: 24 gauge (0.73 mm)
- finish: G60 galvanization with factory-applied primer
- width: 57"

**MgO Facing:**
- thickness: 0.236" (6 mm)
- compliance: ICC-ES AC386 certified
- width: 48"

**EPS Core:**
- meets ASTM C578 Type I
- flame spread rating: ≤25 (ASTM E84)
- smoke development: ≤450 (ASTM E84)

**Adhesive:**
- Type II Class 2 per ASTM D3498
- ICC-ES AC05 compliant

### Structural Properties Evaluated

**Transverse (Bending) Load Capacity:**

Per ESR-4725, the panels carry the following allowable stresses (ASD) for transverse loading:

| Load Case | Design Condition | Capacity (psf) |
|-----------|------------------|-----------------|
| Floor/Roof Positive Wind | L/180, L/240, L/360 | 31.3 |
| Floor/Roof Negative Wind | L/180 deflection limit | 86.7 |
| Floor/Roof Negative Wind | L/240 deflection limit | 86.7 |
| Floor/Roof Negative Wind | L/360 deflection limit | 63.5 |
| Wall Positive Wind | All deflection limits | 38.3 |
| Wall Negative Wind | All deflection limits | 36.6 |

**Axial (Compression) Capacity:**
- wall panels: 2,200 plf (pounds per linear foot) at 113.875" height

**Shearwall Capacity (Seismic & Lateral):**

Seismic Design Category A through C:
- shear capacity: 349 plf at 1:1 aspect ratio
- reduction factor R: 2.0
- overstrength Ω₀: 2.5
- deflection Cd: 2.0
- maximum aspect ratio: 1:1

Seismic Design Category D through F:
- shear capacity: 127 plf at 1:1 aspect ratio
- reduction factor R: 6.5
- overstrength Ω₀: 3.0
- deflection Cd: 4.0
- maximum aspect ratio: 1:1
- maximum height: 65 feet

### Weather Resistance

**Weather Protective Barrier:**
- meets IBC 1402.2 Exception 2
- weather resistance performance is built into the panel construction
- no additional exterior weather protection layer required (panel itself serves as weather barrier)
- complies with air infiltration and moisture control requirements

### Seismic Performance

**Seismic Design Categories A-C:**
- R factor (response modification): 2.0
- Ω₀ (overstrength): 2.5

- allows 1:1 aspect ratio shearwall elements

**Seismic Design Categories D-F (Higher Seismic Risk):**

## Why This Certification Matters for Foundation Design

### For Code Compliance

1. **Proven Structural Equivalency:** The ESR-4725 proves that BOXABL's SIP panels meet or exceed code-required structural performance for:
   - floor and roof bending loads
   - axial compression (wall loads)
   - seismic shear walls
   - wind resistance

2. **Thermal Code Compliance:** The R-values documented in ESR-4725 meet standard thermal codes for the climate zones in most jurisdictions (2021/2018 IBC/IRC).

3. **Fire Safety:** The interior finish and thermal barrier compliance means the panels are recognized as code-compliant for fire safety without additional interior treatments.

4. **Permits and AHJ Acceptance:** Building officials can cite ESR-4725 directly when approving BOXABL construction. It removes much of the "is this factory-built product acceptable?" discussion.

### For Foundation Design

1. **Load Transfer Points:** The structural capacity data tells the foundation engineer exactly what loads the panels can carry and transmit to the foundation. This directly informs:
   - bearing requirements at panel-to-foundation connection points
   - the number and size of anchorage bolts or connection plates needed
   - the foundation design pressure (how much bearing the pad or beams must provide)

2. **Seismic Connection:** In Seismic Categories D-F (where many BOXABL projects are located), the ESR-4725 shearwall capacity (127 plf for D-F) drives:
   - shear wall detailing at panel edges
   - band beam or rim load requirements
   - foundation anchorage spacing and strength

3. **Wind Loads:** The wind capacity (140 mph Exposure B / 120 mph Exposure C standard for BOXABL) must be carried from the roof through the walls to the foundation. The ESR-4725 data confirms the panels can handle it; the foundation must provide the base.

4. **Settlement & Differential Movement:** Because the panels are factory-laminated and uniform, the bearing surface must also be as uniform as possible. Non-uniform settlement can crack panels or separate connections.

## Code Adoption and Jurisdiction Acceptance

**ESR-4725 is recognized in:**
- 2021 IBC (International Building Code)
- 2021 IRC (International Residential Code)
- 2018 IBC and IRC (with minor administrative alignment)
- any jurisdiction that has adopted the IBC or IRC

**Permitting advantage:**
- building officials can approve BOXABL construction directly by referencing ESR-4725
- no need for case-by-case equivalency determinations
- accelerates permit review when the AHJ is familiar with ICC-ES reports

**Note:** Always confirm the jurisdiction has adopted the 2021/2018 IBC or IRC before assuming ESR-4725 acceptance. Some jurisdictions use older codes; in those cases, BOXABL or the engineer may need to provide an equivalency letter.

## Limitations & Clarifications

**ESR-4725 covers:**
- the SIP panel material and properties
- structural capacity and code compliance
- thermal and fire performance

**ESR-4725 does NOT cover:**
- foundation design (that's site-specific)
- utility penetrations (site-specific)
- connections to foundations or other structures (site-specific)
- local amendments or modifications to the panels
- how the panels are assembled on-site into a complete unit

Those elements are the responsibility of the site-specific engineer, BOXABL, or the installation contractor.
