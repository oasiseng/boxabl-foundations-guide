# Glossary

Plain-language definitions for the terms used across this repository. Written for homeowners first, with enough precision for builders and assistants.

**ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit).** A secondary dwelling on a residential lot — a backyard cottage, guest house, or converted structure. Many jurisdictions have streamlined ADU approval paths that small BOXABL units may fit.

**AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction).** The local government body (usually the building department) with the legal power to review plans, issue permits, and inspect construction. The AHJ's interpretation controls, even when manufacturer documents exist.

**Anchorage.** The engineered hardware and connections that tie a structure to its foundation so wind and seismic forces have a complete load path into the ground. Anchorage is always site-specific.

**Bearing capacity.** How much load the soil can safely support, usually expressed in pounds per square foot (psf). Confirmed visually for simple cases or by a geotechnical report; it drives footing and pad sizing.

**CCC / GVWR / UVW.** Towable-RV weight terms (relevant to the Baby Box): GVWR is the maximum loaded weight, UVW is the unit's empty weight, and CCC is the cargo capacity between them.

**Exposure category (B/C/D).** A wind-design classification of the site's surroundings under ASCE 7. Exposure B is sheltered (suburban/wooded), C is open terrain, D is flat open water-adjacent. The same building carries different wind loads in different exposures.

**Finished floor elevation (FFE).** The target height of the completed floor surface relative to grade or a datum. FFE drives drainage, flood compliance, utility stub heights, and step/landing design — lock it early.

**Frost depth.** How deep the ground freezes locally. Footings generally must bear below frost depth to avoid heave. Set by the local code or AHJ.

**Geotechnical report ("geotech").** A soils investigation by a geotechnical engineer establishing bearing capacity, soil type, groundwater, and foundation recommendations. Often required by the AHJ; always decisive when it exists.

**Grade beam.** A reinforced concrete beam at or near grade that provides continuous support lines, often bearing on improved soil or deeper elements.

**ICC-ES / ESR.** ICC Evaluation Service issues Evaluation Service Reports (ESRs) confirming a product meets code requirements. ESR-4725 covers BOXABL's SIP panels. An ESR certifies the product, not your site, foundation, or permit.

**Modular vs. manufactured (HUD) home.** Modular (factory-built) housing is built to the same state/local building codes as site-built homes and sits on a permanent foundation. Manufactured housing is built to the federal HUD code on a permanent chassis. BOXABL Casitas are factory-built/modular products; the Baby Box 120 is a towable RV — a third category. Classification drives the whole permit path.

**PE (Professional Engineer) / wet seal.** A licensed engineer legally responsible for a design. A "wet seal" (or digital seal) on drawings is the PE's formal certification. Many AHJs require sealed, site-specific foundation drawings.

**Pier and beam.** A foundation of discrete concrete piers supporting beams under the structure. Useful on slopes, for minimal disturbance, or where adjustability and under-floor access help.

**Risk category.** An ASCE 7 classification (I–IV) reflecting the consequence of a structure's failure; most homes are Risk Category II. It scales design loads.

**SDC (Seismic Design Category).** A classification (A–F) assigned to a **site** based on location, soils, and risk category — not an inherent property of a building product. A unit can be *rated for sites up to* a given SDC; the site determines what actually applies.

**Setbacks.** Minimum required distances between a structure and property lines, easements, or other structures, set by zoning. Setbacks frequently decide where a unit can physically go.

**SIP (Structural Insulated Panel).** A factory-laminated sandwich panel — facings bonded to an insulating foam core — that carries structural load and provides insulation in one element. BOXABL panels pair a steel facing with an MgO facing over an EPS core.

**Slab-on-grade.** A concrete floor slab poured directly on prepared ground, typically over a gravel base and vapor barrier, with thickened edges or footings at bearing lines. The most common simple-site foundation.

**Stem wall.** A short perimeter concrete or masonry wall rising from a footing, creating a crawlspace and elevation control between the ground and the structure.

**Telehandler.** A telescopic-boom forklift used to lift and position modular units. Delivery planning must confirm the machine can reach, with the unit's weight, from where it can physically stand.

**Vapor barrier.** A membrane (typically polyethylene) under a slab that blocks ground moisture from migrating into the floor assembly. Continuity at penetrations matters more than thickness.
