# Baby Box 120 RV / BXB-000029

This page sets up a **model-specific public briefing** for the BOXABL Baby Box 120 RV Model `BXB-000029`.

It exists because this model should not be treated exactly like a Casita-style permanent foundation lead.

## Official Open Source Position

Official published references checked on **March 16, 2026**:

- [Baby Box page](https://www.boxabl.com/baby-box)
- [Baby Box 120 Spec Sheet (BXB-000029)](https://gcdn.boxabl.com/documents/technical/plan-sets/Baby%20Box%20120%20Spec%20Sheet%20(BXB-000029).pdf)

The official spec sheet describes the Baby Box 120 RV Model as a:

- factory-built **towable recreational vehicle**
- studio floor plan
- integrated chassis product
- unit with adjustable suspension and leveling capability

That matters because the project path may be closer to **site setup, support, leveling, utility hookup, and local placement compliance** than to a conventional permanent foundation package.

## Public Facts That Matter For Lead Intake

Per the official spec sheet, public details include:

- building footprint about `101"` x `168"`
- transport mode about `101"` W x `296"` L x `109"` H
- `GVWR 6,400 lb`
- `UVW 4,400 lb`
- `CCC 2,000 lb`
- `Hitch Weight 50 lb`
- minimum `30 gal` fresh, black, and grey tank capacities
- `30A / 120V` shore power inlet
- `3"` SIP floor, roof, and wall panels at about `R=12`
- `40 psf` floor live load rating
- `20 psf` flat roof live load rating
- `75+ mph` wind speed, `Exposure B/C`

The official setup notes also emphasize:

- on-site electrical hookup
- pre-filling the fresh water tank before travel unless connected to supply
- leveling the unit on-site
- choosing ground that can support the leveling jacks
- verifying utility function before setup completion

## Why This Model Is Different

For a project like this, the first question is not automatically:

- "Which permanent foundation option do we use?"

The first questions are usually:

- Is the jurisdiction treating this as an RV, a temporary placement, or something closer to an accessory structure?
- Is the owner planning short-term placement, long-term placement, or conversion into a more fixed site condition?
- Is the integrated chassis staying in service exactly as designed?
- Is the goal simple site leveling and utility hookup, or a more engineered support and pad condition?
- Does the AHJ want a site plan, support pad design, anchorage, utility plan, or full engineered foundation package?

## What "Foundation Plan" May Mean For Baby Box

For this model, "foundation plan" can mean different things depending on the jurisdiction and owner intent:

### Scenario 1: RV-style placement

The work may be mostly about:

- stable support surface
- leveling strategy
- jack bearing support
- shore power
- water and waste routing
- access and placement clearances

### Scenario 2: Long-term semi-permanent setup

The work may shift toward:

- engineered support pad or bearing zones
- drainage and erosion control
- utility stubs and equipment coordination
- site grading
- tie-down or anchorage questions if required
- local compliance review for prolonged placement
- [Mobile Home Foundation Certifications](https://oasisengineering.com/manufactured-home-foundation-letter/

### Scenario 3: Jurisdiction demands a more engineered answer

The work may escalate into:

- PE-reviewed support or foundation package
- site-specific notes and details
- permit coordination
- utility and elevation coordination
- AHJ response support

## Public-Safe Guidance For This Lead

Do not assume:

- that Casita foundation logic directly applies
- that an RV classification eliminates all site or permit work
- that leveling jacks alone answer all bearing and drainage questions
- that long-term placement will be treated casually by the AHJ

Do assume:

- classification and placement rules must be checked early
- support conditions still matter
- utility planning still matters
- Oasis can add value before full engineering starts

## Recommended Intake For A Baby Box Lead

For this model, collect these first:

- site address and jurisdiction
- intended use: travel, seasonal, guest use, or long-term siting
- expected duration on site
- whether the owner wants wheels/chassis to remain operational exactly as delivered
- grade and drainage condition
- utility plan
- whether the AHJ has already commented on classification or permit path
