## Why The 2-Bedroom Casita Requires Its Own Foundation Path

The 2-Bedroom (BXB-000016) is unique because:

1. **Dual-module complexity.** Unlike single-module models (Studio and 1-Bedroom), this unit arrives as two separate modules that must be positioned, connected structurally, and electrically unified on-site.

2. **Inter-module connection engineering.** The two modules must be joined at a defined interface with structural bolts and an interface plate. This connection must be coordinated with the foundation and verified during installation.

3. **Larger footprint and bearing area.** The combined footprint is roughly 38' x 19' (or similar dual-module layout), requiring more extensive foundation preparation and larger bearing area.

4. **More complex utility coordination.** Utility penetrations are split between modules, and inter-module connections must be coordinated with site utilities.

5. **Delivery logistics constraint.** Two modules mean two crane or telehandler visits, two unfolding sequences, and tighter coordination on site access.

6. **Foundation must support inter-module connection.** The foundation cannot simply be two separate 19' x 19' pads. It must create a unified bearing surface where the two modules meet and connect.

## What "Foundation Plan" Means For 2-Bedroom Casita

For this model, "foundation plan" involves understanding the dual-module reality:

### Multi-Module Foundation Strategy

2-Bedroom projects typically use one of these approaches:

**Unified Concrete Slab:**
- single large slab covering the entire dual-module footprint (approximately 38' x 19' or similar)
- vapor barrier and gravel base beneath entire footprint
- continuous footing at perimeter and possibly additional bearing zones under inter-module interface
- utility penetrations pre-planned for both modules and inter-module connections
- most common approach; simplifies bearing and leveling

**Dual-Slab with Unified Bearing:**
- separate slabs for each module with unified base preparation
- less common; can create settlement differential or leveling challenges
- only acceptable if inter-module interface is explicitly designed for slight movement
- requires clear separation strategy and inspection protocol

**Pier & Beam (Dual Layout):**
- engineered pier layout supporting both modules
- piers positioned to support each module's corner and center loads
- additional piers or beams at inter-module interface
- useful for sloped sites or drainage sensitivity
- requires more complex coordination of pier locations

**Crawlspace with Stem Wall:**
- perimeter stem wall and interior bearing under both modules
- allows elevation control and utilities access
- inter-module connection must cross the crawlspace cleanly
- typical for cooler climates or high water tables

**Grade Beam (Dual-Module):**
- continuous reinforced beam supporting both modules
- useful for sites requiring unified structural strategy
- common when local code favors continuous support lines

### Key Coordination Items for Dual-Module Setup

The installation process for 2-Bedroom units emphasizes:

- **site access for two delivery/unfolding sequences** (module 1, then module 2)
- **utility stub locations in each module** (water, sewer, electrical, gas, vent)
- **inter-module connection point and interface plate location** (must align with foundation)
- **finished floor elevation and slope drainage** (must be consistent between modules)
- **bearing soil capacity under entire footprint** (geotechnical report typical)
- **anchorage connection points in each module** (must support inter-module forces)
- **inter-module electrical and plumbing connections** (coordinated with utilities)
- **local building official review** of inter-module connection details

## Foundation Implications Specific to 2-Bedroom Casita

**Unique Challenges:**

- must design for unified bearing under dual modules (not two independent slabs)
- inter-module connection must be structurally verified and sealed
- settlement must be uniform across both modules to prevent separation
- utility coordination is more complex (dual module stubs + inter-module connections)
- delivery logistics require two crane visits and careful sequencing
- inspection must verify module positioning, connection torque, and utility joins

## Public-Safe Guidance For 2-Bedroom Casita Leads

Do not assume:

- that single-module foundation logic applies to dual-module units
- that two separate 19' x 19' foundation pads are acceptable
- that the inter-module connection is self-evident from factory assembly
- that permitting will be automatic even with manufacturer specs
- that standard ADU or small-structure rules apply to a dual-module unit

Do assume:

- structural and envelope specs are identical to single-module units (same ESR-4725 panels)
- the inter-module connection must be explicitly engineered and verified
- unified bearing and leveling are critical to long-term performance
- geotechnical investigation is strongly recommended
- utility and foundation coordination is more complex than single-module units
- local soil, drainage, and code requirements drive final decisions
- professional inspection of inter-module connections is essential
